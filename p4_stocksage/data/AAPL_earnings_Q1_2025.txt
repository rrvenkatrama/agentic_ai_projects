Apple Q1 2025 Earnings Call Transcript

CEO Tim Cook:
We are incredibly pleased with our Q1 results. Revenue came in at $124.3 billion, up 4% year over year. 
iPhone revenue was $69.1 billion, driven by strong demand for iPhone 16 Pro. 
Services revenue reached an all-time high of $26.3 billion, up 14% year over year.
We now have over 1 billion paid subscriptions across our platform.

On AI: Apple Intelligence is now available on all supported devices. 
We are seeing strong engagement with our AI features including Writing Tools and image generation.
We plan to expand Apple Intelligence capabilities significantly in 2025 with deeper Siri integration.

CFO Luca Maestri:
Gross margin was 46.9%, up from 45.9% last year, reflecting the strength of our Services business.
We returned over $30 billion to shareholders through dividends and buybacks this quarter.
We are guiding Q2 revenue of $88-92 billion, reflecting typical seasonal patterns.
Operating cash flow was $29.9 billion for the quarter.

On China: Greater China revenue was $18.5 billion, down 11% year over year.
We continue to face competitive headwinds in China from local manufacturers.
We remain committed to the China market for the long term.

Analyst Q&A:
Q: What is your outlook for AI monetization?
A: We see AI as a long-term driver of device upgrades and Services growth. 
   Apple Intelligence is already influencing purchase decisions. 
   We expect meaningful revenue contribution from AI features by 2026.

Q: How do you see Services growth trending?
A: We are confident in double-digit Services growth continuing. 
   The installed base is at an all-time high and monetization per user is increasing.
   App Store, Apple TV+, and iCloud are all growing strongly.

Q: What is the impact of tariffs?
A: We are actively managing our supply chain to mitigate tariff impacts.
   India manufacturing is ramping and now supplies a significant portion of US iPhones.
   We do not expect a material impact to our guidance from current tariff levels.
